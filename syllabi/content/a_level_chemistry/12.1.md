| Candidates should be able to: |  |
| 1 explain the lack of reactivity of nitrogen, with reference to triple bond strength and lack of polarity |  |
| 2 describe and explain: (a) the basicity of ammonia, using the Brønsted–Lowry theory (b) the structure of the ammonium ion and its formation by an acid–base reaction (c) the displacement of ammonia from ammonium salts by an acid–base reaction |  |
| 3 state and explain the natural and man-made occurrences of oxides of nitrogen and their catalytic removal from the exhaust gases of internal combustion engines |  |
| 4 understand that atmospheric oxides of nitrogen ($\text{NO}$ and $\text{NO}_2$) can react with unburned hydrocarbons to form peroxyacetyl nitrate, PAN, which is a component of photochemical smog |  |
| 5 describe the role of $\text{NO}$ and $\text{NO}_2$ in the formation of acid rain both directly and in their catalytic role in the oxidation of atmospheric sulfur dioxide |  |

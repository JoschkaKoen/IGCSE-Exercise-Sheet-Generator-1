# 3.5 Integration

1. extend the idea of 'reverse differentiation' to include the integration of $e^{ax + b}$, $\dfrac{1}{ax + b}$, $\sin(ax + b)$, $\cos(ax + b)$, $\sec^2(ax + b)$ and $\dfrac{1}{x^2 + a^2}$
2. use trigonometrical relationships in carrying out integration
3. integrate rational functions by means of decomposition into **partial fractions**
4. recognise an integrand of the form $\dfrac{k\,f'(x)}{f(x)}$, and integrate such functions
5. recognise when an integrand can usefully be regarded as a product, and use **integration by parts**
6. use a given **substitution** to simplify and evaluate either a definite or an indefinite integral

# 1.5 Trigonometry

1. sketch and use graphs of the sine, cosine and tangent functions (for angles of any size, and using either degrees or radians)
2. use the exact values of the sine, cosine and tangent of $30°$, $45°$, $60°$, and related angles
3. use the notations $\sin^{-1}x$, $\cos^{-1}x$, $\tan^{-1}x$ to denote the principal values of the inverse trigonometric relations
4. use the identities $\tan\theta \equiv \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$ and $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta \equiv 1$
5. find all the solutions of simple trigonometrical equations lying in a specified interval

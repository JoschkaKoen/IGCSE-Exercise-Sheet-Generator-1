# 2.2 Matrices

1. formulate a problem involving the solution of 3 linear simultaneous equations in 3 unknowns as a **matrix equation**, or vice versa
2. understand the cases concerning the consistency or inconsistency of 3 linear simultaneous equations, relate them to the singularity of the matrix, solve consistent systems, and interpret geometrically in terms of lines and planes
3. understand the terms '**characteristic equation**', '**eigenvalue**' and '**eigenvector**' as applied to square matrices
4. find eigenvalues and eigenvectors of $2\times 2$ and $3\times 3$ matrices
5. express a square matrix in the form $QDQ^{-1}$, where $D$ is a **diagonal matrix** of eigenvalues and $Q$ is a matrix whose columns are eigenvectors, and use this expression
6. use the fact that a square matrix satisfies its own characteristic equation

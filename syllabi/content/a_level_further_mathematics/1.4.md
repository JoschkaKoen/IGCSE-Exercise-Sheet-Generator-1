# 1.4 Matrices

1. carry out operations of **matrix addition**, **subtraction** and **multiplication**, and recognise the terms **zero matrix** and **identity** (or unit) matrix
2. recall the meaning of 'singular' and 'non-singular' as applied to square matrices and, for $2\times 2$ and $3\times 3$ matrices, evaluate **determinants** and find **inverses** of non-singular matrices
3. understand and use the result $(AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}$ for non-singular matrices
4. understand the use of $2\times 2$ matrices to represent certain **geometric transformations** in the $x$-$y$ plane (including the relationship between $A$ and $A^{-1}$, products $AB$ as composed transformations, the area scale factor as the determinant, and finding the matrix of a given transformation or sequence)
5. understand the meaning of 'invariant' as applied to points and lines under transformations represented by matrices, and solve simple problems involving **invariant points** and **invariant lines**

\section*{What a drug is}

A \textbf{drug} 药物 is any substance taken into the body that changes or affects the \textbf{chemical reactions} 化学反应 in the body. Many drugs are medicines that help to treat illness.

\section*{Antibiotics}

\textbf{Antibiotics} 抗生素 are drugs used to treat bacterial \textbf{infections} 感染. They \textbf{kill bacteria} 细菌, or stop them growing, but they do \textbf{not} affect \textbf{viruses} 病毒. This is why antibiotics cannot cure a cold or the flu, which are caused by viruses.

\section*{Antibiotic resistance}

Some bacteria are \textbf{resistant} 耐药 to an antibiotic — the antibiotic no longer kills them. These bacteria survive and multiply, so the antibiotic slowly becomes less effective for everyone.

\textbf{(Supplement)} To slow this down, antibiotics should be used \textbf{only when they are really needed}, and the full course should always be finished. Using antibiotics too often lets resistant bacteria, such as \textbf{MRSA}, spread.

\section*{Exam tips}

\begin{itemize}
\item A drug is \textbf{any} substance that changes the body's chemical reactions — not only illegal drugs.

\item Antibiotics kill \textbf{bacteria} only; they do \textbf{not} work on \textbf{viruses} (so they are useless against colds and flu).

\item Overusing antibiotics leads to \textbf{resistant} bacteria like MRSA. Use them only when needed, and finish the whole course.

\end{itemize}
